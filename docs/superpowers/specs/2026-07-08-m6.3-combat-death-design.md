# Milestone 6.3 — Combat & Death: Design

*Status: DRAFT for review (2026-07-08). Fourth milestone-6 slice, and the
largest — this is where HP finally changes. Builds on 6.1 (phased move) and 6.2
(monsters, kind, HP, AI). Rebase this branch onto `main` once #14 (6.2) merges.*

## Goal

Turn the "monsters hunt but can't hurt you" world (6.2) into real combat:
**bump-to-attack**, the **attack phase** of the decided phased resolution
(attacks resolve simultaneously against post-move positions), **damage**, and
**death** (HP→0 → monster removed / player respawns). This delivers the payoff
of milestone 6: mutual kills, retreating to dodge, and walking into a monster to
fight it.

## The decided rules this implements (plan §5)

- **Attack phase**: all attacks resolve **simultaneously against post-move
  positions**. Consequences that must fall out: **retreat dodges** (step away and
  the swing finds nothing) and **mutual kills** (two who go for each other can
  both die the same turn).
- **Bump-to-attack**: moving onto a hostile-held hex is an attack — the mover
  stays on its own hex and the move becomes an attack. Checked **after the move
  phase**: if the hostile is still there → attack; if it vacated → the move
  completes into the now-empty hex.
- **Stacking**: friendly stacking up to `StackCap`; **hostiles never share a
  hex**; an attack on a hex with several occupants hits **a random member**.
- **Within a turn, all movement happens first, then all attacks land.**

## Decisions to review (please weigh in)

### 1. Faction = kind
Two factions, derived from `Kind`: **players** and **monsters**. Same faction
stacks (≤ `StackCap`); opposing faction can't share a hex → moving onto it is a
bump-to-attack. Monsters don't fight each other (one hostile faction for now).

### 2. Damage constants (tunable — values are a gameplay call)
New `protocol` constants: **`PlayerAttackDamage = 5`**, **`MonsterAttackDamage =
3`**. With `PlayerMaxHP = 20` / `MonsterMaxHP = 10` (6.2): a player kills a
monster in **2 hits**; a monster takes a player down in **7 hits**. One flat
value per attacker kind — per-class/weapon damage is 6b. **Tell me if you want
different numbers or lethality.**

### 3. Death & respawn (XP penalty deferred)
- **Monster** HP ≤ 0 → **removed** from the world (killed monsters don't respawn
  yet — the pool depletes; continuous spawning is a later tuning pass).
- **Player** HP ≤ 0 → **respawns**: moved to a fresh spawn hex, HP reset to full,
  path cleared, **same entity id + token** (the client stays joined; the next
  snapshot just shows them back at spawn, full HP). The **XP-level fallback**
  (plan's "fall back to the start of your XP level") and any respawn-location
  refinement wait for **6b** (XP doesn't exist yet). Death here has no lasting
  penalty beyond the reset position.

### 4. Monster AI now attacks when adjacent
6.2's AI stopped one hex short of a player. In 6.3 the AI instead **targets the
player's hex when adjacent**, so the move becomes a bump-to-attack. (Approach
behaviour at range 2+ is unchanged.)

### 5. Client — health bars
Damaged entities (`HP < MaxHP`) get a small HP bar over the dot; monsters/players
appearing and dying are already handled by the entity layer's spawn/despawn.
`window.game` exposes entity HP for e2e assertions. A hit/death flash is optional
polish — I'll keep it minimal unless you want it.

### 6. Slice size — keep together or split?
This is the largest slice (combat mechanics + death + client). I **recommend
keeping it as one slice** — combat isn't demonstrable or testable without damage
*and* death, and the client HP bar is small. If you'd rather, we can split into
**6.3a combat mechanics + death (server)** and **6.3b client HP bars**. Say which.

## The combat algorithm (the crux)

`resolveTurn` becomes **think → move (faction-aware, with bump deferral) →
attack → apply damage & deaths**, all under `w.mu`:

1. **Think** (`thinkMonstersLocked`, updated per decision 4): monster paths set,
   now including a step onto an adjacent player's hex.

2. **Move phase** (extends 6.1's seeded phased move to be faction-aware). Process
   movers in the per-turn seeded-shuffled order against an *evolving* board:
   - If the target hex currently holds an **opposing-faction** entity → the mover
     **does not move**; record it as a **pending bumper** (target = that hex),
     path retained.
   - Else if `occ[target] < StackCap` → move (friendly stack / empty hex).
   - Else → wait (same-faction hex full).
   - Because the board evolves as movers resolve, an opposer that already moved
     away frees the hex (the mover takes it instead of bumping); **two opposing
     movers converging on an empty hex** resolve as one taking it and the other
     becoming a pending bumper against it. No opposing co-occupancy ever occurs.

3. **Bump re-check (post-move, for retreat-dodge order-independence)**: for each
   pending bumper, look at its target on the **final** board:
   - target still has an opposing occupant → it's an **attacker** (stays put,
     attacks that hex in the attack phase).
   - target no longer opposing-held (defender vacated) → **cancel the bump**; the
     mover completes into the hex if `occ < StackCap`, else waits. → *retreat
     dodge* / follow-into-vacated.

4. **Attack phase (simultaneous, post-move positions)**: each attacker deals its
   kind's damage to **one random opposing occupant** of its target hex (chosen
   with the per-turn seeded RNG). Accumulate into `damage[entity]` computed
   against **pre-attack HP** — nothing is applied yet, so order doesn't matter.

5. **Apply & deaths**: subtract accumulated damage from each entity's HP; then
   resolve deaths — monsters with HP ≤ 0 are removed; players with HP ≤ 0 respawn
   (decision 3). Applying all damage *before* resolving deaths is what makes
   **mutual kills** work (both blows land even if both fall).

`turn++`.

## Out of scope (later)

- **XP, levels, XP-level death fallback** — 6b.
- **Classes/species, weapon types, ranged (rogue bow) & AoE (mage), auto weapon
  switching** — 6b. 6.3 is flat melee bump-to-attack for everyone.
- **Time bubbles** (mutual-LOS freeze, action-gated turns, walk-in reinforcement,
  the "waiting for…" state) — 6.4.
- **Monster respawn / continuous spawning / spawn balancing** — later tuning.
- **Knockback, status effects, damage variance/crits** — later (crit is an elf
  species trait, 6b).
- **Shared XP-on-kill** — 6b (no XP yet).

## Tests

- **Unit (`internal/game`)**: bump-to-attack deals damage (player steps onto a
  monster → monster HP drops by `PlayerAttackDamage`, attacker stays put);
  **retreat dodges** (defender moves away the same turn → no damage, attacker
  advances into the vacated hex); **mutual kill** (two adjacent opposing entities
  each bump the other, both HP→0, both resolved — monster removed, player
  respawned); **monster death removes it** from the snapshot; **player death
  respawns** with full HP, same id/token, at a walkable hex; **random member**
  (attack on a 2-player stack damages exactly one, seeded/reproducible); AI
  attacks when adjacent (monster adjacent to a player deals damage after a
  resolve). Use `SetSeedForTest` to pin outcomes.
- **Integration (`test/integration`)**: a player who walks into a monster over
  SSE sees the monster's HP fall across turns and disappear when killed; a player
  standing next to a monster loses HP each turn.
- **e2e (`client/e2e`)**: with monsters present, drive a fight and assert (via
  `window.game`) an entity's HP changes; the HP bar renders. Keep the full suite
  green.

## Risks

- **The move-phase extension is the subtle part** — faction-aware bump deferral
  layered onto 6.1's seeded single pass. The invariant to hold: opposing factions
  never co-occupy a hex, and `occ` never exceeds `StackCap`. The single-pass
  ordering has the same accepted imperfection as 6.1 (a bump vs late-vacate can
  depend on shuffle order); the post-move re-check (step 3) makes the common
  retreat-dodge order-independent.
- **Simultaneity/mutual-kill correctness**: damage must be accumulated against
  pre-attack HP and applied together, deaths resolved after — get the ordering
  wrong and mutual kills or same-turn multi-attacker cases break.
- **Respawn must keep id + token** so the client isn't logged out on death.
- **Depends on 6.2 (#14)**: `Kind`, HP fields, `thinkMonstersLocked`,
  `SpawnMonsters`, `MonsterMaxHP`/`PlayerMaxHP` — rebase onto `main` after #14
  merges before building.
