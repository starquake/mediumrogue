# Milestone 6b.2 — Classes: Design

*Status: DRAFT (2026-07-09). Second slice of 6b. Adds the three classes with
distinct combat — the first time weapons, ranged attacks, and AoE exist. Builds
on 6.3 (combat), 6.4 (bubbles), 6b.1 (XP/level). Branch off `main`.*

## Goal

Three playable classes with the decided identities (plan §0):
- **Fighter** — melee only, **tanky** (high HP), medium damage. Holds the front.
- **Rogue** — **dagger** (close) *or* **bow** (ranged), chosen automatically by
  distance; **high single-target damage, squishy**. The mid-liner.
- **Mage** — **staff**: weak melee bonk + **ranged AoE magic**; **squishy**.
  The back line, hitting groups.

## Scope decisions (from the 2026-07-09 discussion)

- **Manual bump + targeted ranged** (your call): melee is still "walk onto the
  adjacent enemy" (6.3 bump); ranged (bow) and AoE (magic) use an **explicit
  ranged attack intent**. Weapons never swap manually — the *weapon* is picked by
  how you engage (bump → close weapon; ranged intent → ranged weapon).
- **All three classes in one slice** (your call).
- **Equipped-weapon model, class defaults only** (your gear discussion): combat
  reads each entity's **equipped close weapon + equipped ranged weapon**, but
  those slots are filled from **class defaults** here. The full **gear system**
  (inventory of multiple items, equip/swap, drops) is **deferred** to a later 6b
  slice — see the `gear-equipment-system` note. Unarmed/fallback rules are baked
  in (below).

## The weapon model

Each entity has two weapon slots, filled by class default:

| Class | Close (bump) | Ranged (intent) |
|---|---|---|
| Fighter | sword (medium) | — none |
| Rogue | dagger (high) | bow (high, range `BowRange`) |
| Mage | staff-bonk (low) | staff magic (AoE, range `MageRange`, radius `MageAoERadius`) |
| *fallback* | **fists** — punch/kick (minimal) when no close weapon | — |

A weapon is `{ damage int; rangeHexes int (0 = melee/adjacent); aoeRadius int (0 =
single target) }`. Damage/stats are server-authoritative; the client shows the
class, not raw weapon math.

## Per-class stats + level scaling

- **MaxHP by class** (level 1): Fighter high (tanky, e.g. **30**), Rogue **16**,
  Mage **14**. (Replaces the flat `PlayerMaxHP=20`.)
- **Weapon damage** (level 1): sword **4**, dagger **7**, bow **6**, staff-bonk
  **2**, staff-magic **4** per target, fists **1**. (Monster `MaxHP=10` → fighter
  ~3 hits, rogue ~2 with dagger, mage ~3 per target but AoE.)
- **Level scaling** (6b.1 levels now matter): `MaxHP += HPPerLevel*(level-1)`;
  weapon damage `+= DamagePerLevel*(level-1)` (or every N levels). A single
  `statsFor(class, level)` / `weaponDamage(weapon, level)` helper is the one
  source of truth (used by combat, spawn/respawn HP, and the wire).

*All numbers above are tunable constants in `internal/protocol` — flagged for
your review; the mechanics are the substance.*

## Attack resolution (extends 6.3/6.4 combat)

Combat still resolves inside bubbles (6.4): think → move (with bump) → attack →
damage & deaths. Changes:

- **Melee bump** (unchanged flow): a move onto an adjacent enemy deals the
  attacker's **close-weapon** damage (class default; fighter sword, rogue dagger,
  mage staff-bonk, or fists), scaled by level.
- **Ranged attack intent** (NEW): a player with a **ranged** weapon submits
  `AttackIntent{target hex}` instead of a move for that turn. In the attack phase
  (against post-move positions):
  - **Bow** (rogue): single-target — damage the opposing entity at the target hex
    (a stack → a random member, per the 6.3 rule), if within `BowRange` and there
    is a hostile there.
  - **Magic** (mage): **AoE** — damage every opposing-faction entity within
    `MageAoERadius` of the target hex, if the target is within `MageRange`.
  - Accumulated with bump damage against pre-attack HP (simultaneous — mutual
    kills / retreat-dodge still hold).

## Ranged rules (the doc's open §9 questions — decided here)

1. **Range**: `BowRange = 4`, `MageRange = 4` (hexes). `MageAoERadius = 1` (the
   target hex + its 6 neighbours).
2. **Line-of-sight**: **distance-only** — no terrain-blocked LOS (consistent with
   the distance-based bubble choice; terrain-LOS is a shared deferred follow-up).
3. **Friendly fire**: **none** — bow/magic only hit **opposing-faction** entities
   (co-op game; no hitting your own party). A bow into a mixed stack hits a random
   *hostile* member.
4. **Where**: ranged attacks resolve during a **bubble** combat turn (you must be
   in a fight, target within range). Submitting a ranged intent counts as your
   lock-in for the bubble (6.4 gating).

## Wire (`internal/protocol`)

- `Class` constants (`ClassFighter`/`ClassRogue`/`ClassMage`) + `Entity.Class`
  (json `class`).
- `JoinRequest.Class` — the class chosen at character creation (default
  `ClassFighter` if empty/unknown, so old clients still work).
- A ranged attack intent: extend `IntentRequest` with a `Kind`
  (`"move"`/`"attack"`) + reuse `Target`, **or** a separate `AttackRequest`
  {`EntityID`, `Token`, `Target`}. (Implementer picks the cleaner fit; keep it
  minimal.)
- Per-class weapon/stat constants (HP, damages, ranges, AoE, scaling).

## Client (`client/`)

- **Class picker at join**: three buttons (Fighter/Rogue/Mage) before/at claim;
  sends the class in the join. Persist the choice with the identity.
- **Render class**: a per-class marker/tint on the dot (so you can tell classes
  apart) — keep it light.
- **Ranged attack UX (in combat)**: when in a bubble and you have a ranged weapon,
  clicking a hostile within range issues a **ranged attack intent** (bow); for the
  mage, clicking a hex issues an **AoE** there; clicking empty ground still
  **moves**. A small affordance shows attack-vs-move. Out of combat, clicks move.
- `window.game` exposes `class` (and enough to drive the e2e: whether a ranged
  attack landed / an enemy took ranged damage).

## Out of scope (later)

- **Gear/inventory/equip/drops** — deferred (`gear-equipment-system` note); 6b.2
  uses class defaults.
- **Species** (human +XP, elf +crit, dwarf +DR) — 6b.3.
- **Terrain-blocked LOS** for ranged — shared deferred follow-up.
- **Weapon/magic variety within a lane** ("different kinds of magic") — the gear
  slice.

## Tests

- **Unit (`internal/game`)**: per-class MaxHP + level scaling (a level-5 fighter
  has more HP than level 1); bump deals the class's close-weapon damage (fighter
  sword vs rogue dagger differ); a rogue **bow** intent damages a hostile at range
  (within `BowRange`, out of melee) and misses out of range; a mage **AoE**
  damages *all* hostiles in radius (2 monsters in radius both take damage) but
  **no friendly fire** (a co-located player unharmed); fighter cannot ranged-attack
  (rejected). Pin seeds.
- **Integration**: join as each class (HP differs on the wire); a rogue kills a
  monster at range over HTTP; a mage AoE damages two monsters.
- **e2e**: pick a class at join → `window.game.class`; the class marker renders;
  (combat server) a ranged attack lands (an enemy's HP drops from range).

## Risks

- **Big slice** — the plan decomposes it into protocol → stats/class → melee
  weapons → ranged intent → AoE → client → tests. Natural cut line if it's too
  much: ranged/AoE (server) vs the client targeting UX.
- **Ranged intent vs move-intent** must not break the 6.4 lock-in/gating (a ranged
  intent is a valid lock-in); and ranged attacks must respect domain scoping
  (target must be in the same bubble/domain).
- **Level-scaling one source of truth** (`statsFor`) so HP/damage/wire agree —
  same discipline as 6b.1's `levelFor`.
- **Backward-compatible join** (default class) so nothing breaks existing
  join/e2e that don't send a class.
